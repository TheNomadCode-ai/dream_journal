Dream memories are fragile. The transition from REM to wakefulness creates a narrow retention window where details can evaporate quickly.

At Somnia, we designed our capture flow around a practical fact: urgency improves recall. If you create a repeatable wake routine, your dream memory gets stronger week over week.

## Day 1-2: Reduce Morning Friction

Place your phone where you can reach it without standing up. Avoid checking messages before writing. Open your journal first.

## Day 3-4: Capture Fragments, Not Stories

Most people fail because they aim for complete narratives. Instead, write fragments: characters, places, emotions, and symbols.

## Day 5: Add Emotional Labeling

After writing your dream, add one emotional tag. Emotional salience strengthens retrieval pathways when you review entries later.

## Day 6: Review Before Sleep

Read your last two dream entries before bed. This primes your attention system to notice and retain dream content during the next sleep cycle.

## Day 7: Identify Recurring Symbols

Scan your week for repeated motifs. Recurring symbols are often your strongest anchors and make future dreams easier to remember.

A dream journal works best when capture is immediate, lightweight, and consistent. Keep the ritual simple, and your recall will compound.
