# Somnia — User Personas (SP-02)

---

## Persona 1 · Maya Chen

**Age:** 28
**Occupation:** UX Designer at a mid-size tech company
**Tech Comfort:** High — uses 15+ apps daily, early adopter, iOS-first

### Primary Goal
Maya wants to understand the emotional undercurrents of her recurring dreams, particularly anxiety dreams about work, and use that insight to inform her stress management routine.

### Top 3 Frustrations with Existing Solutions
1. **No pattern analysis** — she logs dreams in Notion but never surfaces trends without manually re-reading months of entries.
2. **Too much friction in the morning** — by the time she's opened Notion, located her template, and started typing, the dream has faded.
3. **Privacy anxiety** — she's written deeply personal entries and later felt uncomfortable when an app updated its privacy policy to allow "anonymized data sharing."

### Devices & Usage Context
- iPhone 15 Pro, M2 MacBook Pro
- Opens app within 45 seconds of waking — phone already on nightstand
- Reviews entries on desktop once a week for reflection

### Willingness to Pay
**$8–12/month** — comfortable paying for tools she uses daily. Would prefer annual billing for the discount. Not interested in lifetime deals ("what if the app dies?").

### Quote
> "I already know I have the anxiety dream when deadlines pile up — I just want the app to *show* me that pattern so I can actually do something about it."

## Persona 2 · Daniel Okafor

**Age:** 42
**Occupation:** High school English teacher, hobby novelist
**Tech Comfort:** Medium — comfortable with web apps; avoids complex setups; Android user

### Primary Goal
Daniel uses dreams as creative raw material for his fiction. He wants a searchable archive of dream imagery, characters, and plot fragments he can mine when he sits down to write.

### Top 3 Frustrations with Existing Solutions
1. **No rich search** — he has 400+ entries in a paper journal he can't search; digital apps with search either lack it or it's too basic.
2. **Poor tagging** — he wants to tag by character name, symbol, setting (e.g., "the red house") but apps only support flat mood tags.
3. **Export lock-in** — he tried DayOne but fears his creative archive is trapped in a proprietary format.

### Devices & Usage Context
- Samsung Galaxy S23, Windows laptop
- Logs dreams on his phone in bed, then adds tags and notes on his laptop during a free period at school
- Uses the app 5–6 days per week during school terms, every day during holidays

### Willingness to Pay
**One-time Lifetime deal preferred** — teachers budget carefully; doesn't love recurring subscriptions. Would pay **$40–$60** for lifetime access if the app felt stable and trustworthy.

### Quote
> "My dreams are my sketchbook. I just need a version that I can search, that won't disappear, and that I actually own."

## Persona 3 · Sara Lindqvist

**Age:** 35
**Occupation:** Somatic therapist in private practice
**Tech Comfort:** Low-medium — uses iPhone for personal life, avoids anything that needs setup; values simplicity above features

### Primary Goal
Sara practices Jungian-influenced therapy and keeps a personal dream journal as part of her own ongoing self-development work, distinct from her clinical notes. She wants a simple, calm tool that gets out of the way.

### Top 3 Frustrations with Existing Solutions
1. **Over-engineered UI** — most apps feel designed for productivity power-users; she wants a blank page with a good font, not a dashboard.
2. **Pushy AI suggestions** — she tried Reflectly and found the AI prompts ("How did this make you feel?") intrusive and prescriptive.
3. **Notification creep** — apps that start nudging her daily "Don't forget to log your dream!" undermine the voluntary, sacred quality of the practice.

### Devices & Usage Context
- iPhone 14, iPad Pro (uses for reading)
- Logs dreams sitting in bed with morning tea, unhurried — 10–20 minutes after waking
- Revisits entries once per month for personal reflection, sometimes shares anonymous excerpts with her Jungian study group

### Willingness to Pay
**$5–7/month** — thoughtful about subscriptions; would pay more for strong privacy guarantees. More likely to convert after a 30-day free trial than from a paywall.

### Quote
> "I'm not looking for the app to tell me what my dreams mean. I need a quiet container — like a beautiful notebook that happens to be searchable."
